How to Protect Yourself and Others

Avoid close contact

Avoid close contact with people who are sick. Put 6 feet of distance between yourself and people who don’t live in your household.

The risks of getting COVID-19 are higher. Especially in crowded and inadequately ventilated spaces.

To limit exposure, health organizations are urging people to keep safe distances from each other and avoid touching.

Cover your mouth

Wear a mask. Cover coughs and sneezes. You can cough or sneeze into your mask. Put on a new, clean mask, and wash your hands.

People who haven’t been fully vaccinated should continue wear face masks in indoor public places and outdoors.

The CDC guidance is people continue wear face masks in hospitals, clinics, doctors’ offices, etc.

Clean your hands

Wash hands with soap and water for at least 20 seconds after being in a public place or after blowing your nose, coughing, or sneezing.

This eliminates germs including viruses that may be on hands. Hands touch many surfaces and can pick up viruses.

This is one of the most important ways to prevent infection if you haven’t seen your healthcare workers clean hands.
