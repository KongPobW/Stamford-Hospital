Do not stay glued to the news

If you are staying at home, it might feel tempting to spend to spend a lot of time scrolling through social media and the news.

If this leads to difficult feelings, avoid paying attention to things that makes you feel worried or left out, focus on positive distractions instead.

Always use trustworthy sources, such as GOV.UK or the NHS website and fact-check information from the news, social media, or other people.

Whatever your struggles are, you aren't alone. There is help out there, so please don’t be afraid to take advantage of it. There is no shame in needing extra support.

If you are worrying about finances, health, or loved ones, that is expected. This section provides ten top tips to support mental wellbeing while staying at home during the pandemic.

If you don’t know where to begin, every mind matters for young people is a fantastic hub that will guide you to the most appropriate resources for your needs.
